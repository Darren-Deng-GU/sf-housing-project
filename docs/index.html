The Problem

The San Francisco Bay Area faces one of the most severe housing affordability crises in the United States. Median home prices exceed $1.3 million in San Francisco, while median rents for a one-bedroom apartment top $3,000 per month. The gap between housing demand and supply continues to widen, fueled by population growth, tech-sector employment, and constrained land availability.

A critical but often overlooked bottleneck is the building permit process. How quickly permits are approved—and which neighborhoods receive the most development activity—directly affects whether new housing units can keep pace with demand. Understanding these patterns is essential for policymakers seeking to improve housing supply through regulatory reform.

Research Questions

This project investigates three interconnected policy questions:

Permit Efficiency

How long does it take for building permits to move from filing to issuance, and how does this vary by permit type and neighborhood?

Supply & Demand

Which neighborhoods are receiving the most development activity, and how does this correlate with housing costs and demographic patterns?

Land Use Evolution

How is land use changing across San Francisco? What types of conversions (commercial to residential, etc.) are most common?
