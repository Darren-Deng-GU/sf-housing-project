The Problem

The San Francisco Bay Area faces one of the most severe housing affordability crises in the United States. Median home prices in San Francisco exceed $1.3 million. Median rent for a one-bedroom apartment tops $3,000 per month. The gap between housing demand and supply keeps widening, driven by population growth, tech-sector hiring, and limited developable land.

One often overlooked bottleneck is the building permit process. The speed of approvals and the geography of development activity both determine whether new housing can keep pace with demand. Understanding these patterns helps policymakers design better regulatory reforms.

Research Questions

This project investigates three connected policy questions:

Permit Efficiency

How long do permits take to move from filing to issuance? How does that vary by permit type and neighborhood?

Supply & Demand

Which neighborhoods see the most development? How does that line up with local housing costs and demographics?

Land Use Evolution

How is land use changing across San Francisco? Which conversions, such as commercial to residential, are most common?
